About Me


My traditional morning starts and ends on the slopes, I work tirelessly to improve my skiing technique, and I am convinced that success can only be achieved in the work that I truly enjoy!

My name is Stanislav Polishchuk, I am a ski school instructor⛷
My story began with Bukovel Ski School in the 2019-2020 season. At that moment, I had the specialty "Civil Engineering" behind my shoulders, which also brought the necessary contribution to my life path.

My ideal day, I would describe a day at skischoolbukovel - active and full, necessarily in the circle of colleagues who have become friends for a long time. And although the winter season does not last 12 months, I return to Bukovel every year with great pleasure. I always strive for the best, so my team and I constantly get even more opportunities for personal development.













Skiing

"A miracle sport for generative happiness"

Non-skiers often ask why skiers will drive seven hours to a mountain the distance from my Stockton home to Lake Tahoe after work on Friday, then back on Sunday night; why we sleep in our cars if accommodations are not available, eat sack lunches, and spend all our disposable income just to ski as many days as possible. The words “ski” and “mountain” form an expression in the phrase “ski the mountain,” which, when put into action, generates the answer to the mysterious dedication of skiers, “healthy, happy life,” or, as translated into the most celebrated idiom in skiing, “Living the dream.”

For decades, ski and fitness magazines have enthusiastically boasted important health benefits to downhill skiing. Most of these are familiar, obvious and strong incentives for getting out on the slopes. However, if we consider these benefits in conjunction with new scientific research in several related fields of inquiry, skiing becomes a miracle sport for people looking for a natural way to better not only their physical and mental health but also their “prosocial” capacities.
